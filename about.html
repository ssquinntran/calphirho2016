PHI SIGMA RHO

ABOUT

Phi Sigma Rho is a social sorority for women in technical studies serving our sisters and the community by promoting:

  • High standards of personal integrity, respect, and character
  • Lifelong bonds of sisterhood
  • Academic and professional excellence with a social balance
  • Through shared experiences, common bonds, and recognition of service and achievement

Founded in 1984, Phi Sigma Rho is a national sorority for women in engineering and engineering technologies with over twenty-five chapters nationwide. In addition, we support the Leukemia and Lymphoma Society. The Alpha Theta Chapter of Phi Sigma Rho was created by three lovely ladies: Annie Cheng (EECS '17), Anusha Jain (EECS '16), and Rachel Zhang (EECS '17). During their first year, it was important to them to find a support system with whom they could relate and would understood the academic vigor of Berkeley STEM. Unable to find an organization with the perfect balance of academic and social values, they decided to create their own. The result is a community—a sisterhood—with the purpose of bringing out each other's potential and providing academic and moral support. This chapter has a commitment to empowerment and service. As an organization under the Multi Cultural Greek Council, we believe strive to foster values of mutual respect and unity.
